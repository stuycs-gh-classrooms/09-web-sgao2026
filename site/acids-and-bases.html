Unit 12: Acids and Bases

Determining an Acid or Base

not always reliable

  • taste
    • acid: sour
    • base: bitter or soapy
  • touch
    • dilute acid: feels like water
    • concentrated acid: stinging or itchy
    • dilute base: slippery
    • concentrated base: slippery
  • acids are metal reaction (single replacement reaction) while bases tend not to react with metal
  • both will conduct when dissolved in water as both will dissociate

Definitions

what makes an acid an acid and a base a base is not always clear

  • arrhenius - a(n) acid/base will dissolve and dissociate in water to give H+/OH- as its only cation/anion
  • bronsted-lowry - a(n) acid/base is a proton donor/acceptor
  • lewis - a(n) acid/base is an electron acceptor/donor
  • amphoteric - substances that can act as either an acid or a base
