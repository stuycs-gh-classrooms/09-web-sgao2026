study guide

Concentration Curve

must involve reversible reactions

  • equilibrium point - concentration constant, rates equal
  • product favored equilibrium - higher concentration of product at equilibrium
  • reactant favored equilibrium - higher concentration of reactants at equilibrium
  • kinetics will change the slope/speed of reaction but will not change the ending state
resource

Equilibrium Constant

can be derived directly from reaction equation (K)

  • can be in concentration and pressure units
  • high constant = product favored
  • low constant = reactant favored
  • temperature must be constant
resource

Reaction Quotient

can be measured at any point

  • same equation as equilibrium constant
  • if Q = K, reaction at equilibrium, will not progress; DOES NOT STOP
  • if Q > K, reaction will shift to reactants
  • if Q < K,reaction will shift to the products
resource

ICE Chart

also known as RICE Chart

  • 5% assumption - if quadratic, ignore x in denominator and solve; if solution less that 5% than the constant, accept the solution
